Overview

The Tiger, Panthera tigris, is the largest cat species alive today. With male tigers varying from 250 to 390 cm and 90 and 300 kg, they are fierce apex predators.

Due to deforestation and poaching, Panthera tigris only lives in 6% of its historical range. In 2014, there were 2226 tigers in India. Due to national conservation projects such as Project Tiger, it is one of the only countries which has an increasing tiger population
